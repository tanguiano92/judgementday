Judgement Day: Artist Statement

One of my earliest memories of my introduction of Catholicism was of my Abuela's church priest
explaining to her and I about how children are born inherently as sinners and it is up to
him and the adults in our lives to keep us on the right path in life so we can someday make
it to heaven when we die. I remember being puzzled by this statement as I was only seven-years
-old and as far as I knew, I was not a bad child. I was taught a year later that my being a girl
child meant my soul was even in more danger of falling victim to evil forces than that of boys my
age. If I wanted to go to heaven one day, I could not want for false idols, I had to stay pure in body,
mind and spirit until marriage, I had to follow my father's rules until I was passed on to a husband
and I had to do my duty of being a stay-at-home mother who blindly followed whatever demands my future
husband would have for me when the time came. I was eight when I began to question those rules and statements.
They didn't make sense to me. I knew I was being groomed by an adult male priest and my father's family to be
afraid of myself and learn that I could not be trusted with my freedom, choices, and life. When I was thirteen
I decided for myself to stray away from the toxic brand of Catholicism that had been fed to me as a child.
I became a witch. I converted into pagainsim and when my Catholic family found out, I was kicked out of my
family home until I repented and go back to church. However, after weeks of refusing to return to Catholicism,
and as a last ditch attempt to reel me back in, my family called me up and asked me to talk to their parish priest
about my decision. In which I refused. Later on in life, I found out that my family were going to have an exorcism
conducted on me because my family was terrified that my leaving the religion would mean I would not go to heaven
with them when we are all gone. I eventually went back home and my father had no choice but accept my new beliefs
but I often think about what would have happened if I agreed to go with my family to the church that day so long ago.
How far would they have taken the encounter? What would I have been put through? All because they were afraid of
my judgement day. They thought they were going to save me from myself and damnation. I never considered my free
will would be a threat. Once I unlearned what had been forced onto me as a child and I no longer wonder about whether
or not I will go to heaven or hell after I die, I wonder now where I will go? I understand that my experience does
not speak for everyone's experience with this religion, however I began poking holes and questioning the things that
were said to me by adults at church. I created "Judgement Day" as a therapy piece for myself and also to follow
my theme of the afterlife. The aspect of there being a crossroads and depending on which path you choose will
determine whether you go to heaven or hell. To me, the path to heaven was boring and I was never going to walk
down that trail. I don't believe in hell or heaven anymore but if I had to choose which way to go, well, I will
embrace my sinner soul if it means I can live for myself and not be prepped to only be a mother and wife.
